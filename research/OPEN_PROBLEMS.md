# Open Problems

## Use of This Register

An entry belongs here when the repository lacks a satisfactory construction,
model, proof, citation, implementation, or counterexample analysis. “Candidate
next evidence” is deliberately smaller than “solve the whole chapter.” No item
below is claimed novel; literature review is a prerequisite for novelty status.

## Preserved Failed or Incomplete Approaches

- Poker backup's “withhold cards from their owners to prevent association”
  conflicts directly with informed betting. Preserve it as an explicit
  privacy/functionality tradeoff, not as an implementation TODO.
- Trust Establishment's local-randomness pipeline can derive a local secret but
  does not establish peer identity or a shared authenticated key by itself.
- Tic-Tac-Toe's Monte Carlo loop cannot establish an exhaustive non-loss proof
  without a coverage argument; repeated responses may reveal the policy.
- The completed public exhaustive checker closes only the disclosed-input
  baseline. It deliberately reveals the table and therefore cannot be reported
  as a solution to hidden strategy, proof of knowledge, or anti-cloning.
- A bare `hash(y)` does not hide low-entropy reasons or prove policy compliance.
- General proof that all copies were deleted is not available without strong
  control/attestation assumptions; cryptographic erasure is a narrower goal.
